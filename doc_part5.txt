5 RULES OF SAFE OPERATION AND MAIN METHODS 
 
5.1 General requirements 
 
The operation of Rooppur NPP Unit 1 shall be carried out in strict compliance with the requirements of this Technical Specification, 
the Design Safety Analysis Report (SAR), and operating instructions. Any deviations from the limits and conditions of safe operation 
specified herein are considered violations and must be reported immediately to the Bangladesh Atomic Energy Regulatory Authority 
(BAERA) and the NPP Chief Engineer. 
 
All operational personnel of the Unit (including Shift Supervisors, Reactor Control Operators, Turbine Control Operators) must undergo 
regular training, pass qualifications, and hold valid licenses for operating NPP systems. 
 
5.2 Core loading and refueling rules 
 
1) The reactor core loading and refueling operations shall be performed only in the “Refueling” state (CSt or CD, with the reactor 
pressure vessel open and the refueling pit flooded with boric acid solution). 
2) The boric acid concentration in the refueling pit and fuel pool shall be maintained at ≥ 16.0 g/kg at all times during fuel handling. 
3) Continuous neutron flux monitoring shall be provided in the source range (using JKT instrumentation). If neutron monitoring is lost, 
all fuel handling operations must be stopped immediately. 
4) The refueling machine (10FAK30) must be fully tested, calibrated, and have all safety interlocks active. 
5) Only approved fuel assemblies (FA) in accordance with the core loading scheme shall be loaded. Loading of damaged FA is 
prohibited. 
 
5.3 Reactor start-up and power ascension rules 
 
1) Reactor start-up (transition from subcritical state to critical state and power ascension) is allowed only if all safety systems are fully 
operable and in standby readiness. 
2) During start-up, the rate of reactor power increase (neutron flux increase period) must be monitored and maintained at > 20 seconds. 
3) The transition from the “reaching MCPL” state to the “Power Operation” state is performed by pulling out the control rods in a 
strict sequence defined by the design. 
4) The rate of primary circuit heat-up must not exceed 10 °C per hour. 
5) Transition to 100% Nnom is performed in steps of 5-10% of nominal power with stabilization and chemical parameter checks.

5.4 Power operation rules 
 
1) Power operation is allowed at thermal power levels up to 1200 MW (100% Nnom). 
2) Steady power operation must be maintained with automatic control systems (reactor power regulator, turbine speed controller, 
and steam generator level controllers) in active mode. 
3) Primary circuit pressure, temperatures at reactor inlet/outlet, and pressurizer level must be monitored and kept within normal operation 
parameters (Table 4.4). 
4) The chemical composition of the primary and secondary circuit water must be monitored continuously and kept within normative 
limits (Table 4.7). 
5) Routine maintenance of safety systems is allowed only within the permissible repair times (72 or 192 hours) specified in Table 3.8. 
 
5.5 Shutdown and cooldown rules 
 
1) Scheduled reactor shutdown is performed by inserting the control rods (by GCS switch or automatic power reduction controller). 
2) Emergency reactor shutdown (reactor trip) is initiated automatically by safety system settings or manually by pressing the EP 
button on the MCR panel in case of any unsafe deviations. 
3) After reactor shutdown, the decay heat removal is performed initially by steam dumping to the turbine condenser (via BRU-K) or 
to the atmosphere (via BRU-A). 
4) Cooldown of the primary circuit must be performed at a rate not exceeding 15 °C per hour. 
5) The active cooldown using the JNA system is initiated when the primary circuit temperature drops below 140 °C and pressure 
drops below 4.4 MPa. 
6) During cooldown, the boric acid concentration in the primary circuit must be increased to ≥ 16.0 g/kg before the primary circuit 
temperature drops below 100 °C.
